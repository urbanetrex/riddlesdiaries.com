Kryal Castle, diary

This is me exploring the wonders of Kryal Castle!

It is a medieval-themed park located in Ballarat, Victoria, Australia. The park features a replica castle, themed attractions, and live performances that bring the medieval era to life.

For more information and to buy tickets, visit the official website.

On the way...

Our buses travelled to the M8 highway, and I saw some people are already passing paper images, drawings, and messages inside the bus.

I saw some signposts with "Ballarat" engraved on it, sometimes also with "M8".

Suddenly, the bus took a sharp jerk. I guess we were entering Forbes Road, according to Google Maps I viewed the last day.

After a few minutes, someone started to shout "Kryal Castle! Kryal Castle!" and everyone started to chant together. We were all so excited to finally arrive at our destination!

After another minute, I saw Kryal Castle. Our bus parked beside the other bus we had just met at the school.

As we disembarked, the excitement was palpable, and we could see the castle's towers rising in the distance!

Arrival at Kryal Castle

We crossed a draw bridge before entering the castle, just like a real medieval castle. I even saw a real moat!

Some were exclaiming "Wow! Look at that!" as we approached the entrance.

Some assistants left their jobs to tour us (probably they knew we were coming because our school contacted them.)

We first gathered at an assembly point, under a big shade building. We left our bags there. The assistants explained the day's activities and rules to us.

Only our grade are coming, and there were four classes. We headed for different activities.

Our class first headed to the archery shed.

Archery

As we entered the shed, I saw a board saying the arrows' prices were displayed... but since our school must've had contacted them, we ignored them... but I still used them for jokes.

We were all eager to try our hand at archery, and the instructors were very friendly, explaining the safety rules and how to properly use the bows and arrows.

We had 3 turns each. It actually turned out to be a lot of fun.

After our turns, we gathered to share our experiences and see who hit the target the best!

I also noticed that the targets are painted on hay and the border is made of hay blocks too... but I saw something interesting: Five arrows FLEW INTO THE ROOF!!!

Leather

After archery, we headed to some open tables with some tools on them.

We learned about the different types of leather and how it is processed.

But leather can be made of urine... and human skin too...

It is a surprising fact that highlights the various materials that can be used in leather production.

We also got to try our hand at making small leather items, which was a fun and creative activity.

An assistant got out some leather pieces, bowls of water, and some cloth.

We learned how to shape the leather and create simple designs.

Afterward, the assistant gave our leather punched holes for hanging.

We were excited to use the leather pieces for our crafts!

Lunch

I forgot where I should put this section, here or after the next section?

After our activities, we gathered for lunch, sharing stories about our experiences at Kryal Castle.

We enjoyed sandwiches, fruit, and drinks while discussing our favorite parts of the day.

Everyone was excited to share their archery scores and leather creations!

It was a great time to relax and recharge before the next adventure.

Medicine

Our next adventure was going to be more interesting!

We headed to another shed, where we have ceramic mixing bowls and sticks.

We learned about medieval medicine and how it was practiced during that time.

We learned about the different herbs and plants used for healing, and how they were divpared.

We divided to 4 or 6 groups, and each group went to a table with a mixing bowl and a mixing stick. The assistant who explained to us about the medicine got some herbs and showed us how to mix them properly. He handed us some different kinds of herbs and let us try mixing them ourselves!

He also expected them to be sticky, and came around to check.

We were all fascinated by the different scents and textures of the herbs, and it was a fun and educational experience.

The Battle of Hastings

We were now reviewing the Battle of Hastings on an open sandy ground.

The Battle of Hastings took place in 1066 and was a pivotal moment in English history.

It marked the beginning of Norman rule in England and had lasting effects on the culture and governance of the country.

We learned about the key figures involved in the battle, including William the Conqueror and King Harold II.

This assistant also chose some volunteers to demo, and she got a fake CROWN to also show the king!

Then she got us to take some foam shields and swords to practice, but also warned of the swords' tip is actually not foam, but hard plastic.

We practiced some basic sword fighting techniques, which was a lot of fun.

And then the next one will be the last learning activity before recess...

Dance

Our classes gathered together on some rocky ground in front of the shade to dance now!

We planned to learn some medieval dances, but ended up only learning one.

We grouped into two big circles, including our class teachers and some assistants.

It was a fun experience, and we all enjoyed trying to follow the steps!

We then danced with the music, and the music accelerated to more than 3.0x! The laughter got larger as it accelerated!

Recess

After the dance, we stayed under the shade. I asked my teacher about the maze ten times.

We chatted and ate, just like in lunch break.

It was a nice way to wind down after a fun-filled day!

I then spent the time writing what I learned to the notebook.
